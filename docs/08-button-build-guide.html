Before You Start

Tools needed

  • Soldering iron + solder
  • Wire strippers
  • Multimeter
  • Heat gun (or lighter) for heat shrink
  • USB-C cable (for flashing)
  • Laptop with ESPHome dashboard open
  • Tweezers & isopropyl alcohol + cotton swab

GPIO Pin Assignments — Memorize These

FunctionXIAO Pin LabelGPIO #
Tact switch inputD1GPIO1
Passive buzzer PWMD2GPIO2
Battery positiveBAT+ padnear D5 silkscreen (back of board)
Battery negativeBAT- padnear D8 silkscreen (back of board)
⚠️ GPIO3 is NOT D1. GPIO3 is the RF switch power pin — an internal board signal. Do not use it. The correct mapping is D1 = GPIO1, D2 = GPIO2. The full XIAO pin map: D0=GPIO0, D1=GPIO1, D2=GPIO2, D3=GPIO21, D4=GPIO22, D5=GPIO23.

1 Inspect the XIAO board

  • ☐ No bent pins, no visible damage
  • ☐ USB-C port is clean
  • ☐ Find the BAT+ pad on back of board (near D5 label)
  • ☐ Find the BAT- pad on back of board (near D8 label)

2 Prep the LiPo battery

⚠️ LiPo batteries can catch fire if shorted. The two bare wire ends must never touch each other — insulate them immediately after cutting.
  1. Cut off the JST connector leaving ~5cm of wire attached to the battery
  2. Immediately slide a piece of heat shrink over each wire end and shrink it down — leave only ~4mm of wire exposed at the tip. Do this before anything else. Both ends insulated before you proceed.
  3. Strip the 4mm tips
  4. Tin both wire tips with solder

3 Solder battery to XIAO

  1. Tin the BAT+ and BAT- pads on the back of the XIAO
  2. Verify polarity with multimeter before soldering (red = +, black = −, but verify — some units are reversed)
  3. Solder red wire to BAT+ pad
  4. Solder black wire to BAT- pad
  5. Check that no wire strands are poking sideways toward adjacent pads
  6. Tug gently — joints should not move
Note: no heat shrink needed on the board side — the solder pad is the joint. The heat shrink from Step 2 protects the battery-end wires from shorting against each other.

✅ Check: Plug in USB-C. Orange charge LED on XIAO should light up.

4 Prep the tact switch

  1. Cut two ~2.5" wires (red + black)
  2. Solder red wire to any pin on one side of the switch
  3. Solder black wire to any pin on the opposite side
  4. (The 4 pins are connected in pairs — you're bridging across the switch)

5 Solder switch to XIAO

  1. Solder red wire → D1 pin on XIAO
  2. Solder black wire → any GND pin on XIAO
✅ Check (optional — skip if you don't have alligator clip leads): The GDT-311 has no continuity beeper — use Ω mode, 200 range instead. Red probe to D1 wire, black probe to GND wire. Unpressed = "1." (open circuit). Pressed = near 0. You need both hands to hold probes + one to press — use alligator clip leads or a helping-hands stand, or just skip and verify during the firmware test in Step 10.

6 Prep the buzzer

  1. Cut two ~2.5" wires
  2. Solder one wire to one pin, other wire to the other pin — polarity doesn't matter for a passive buzzer, it's driven by PWM not DC

7 Solder buzzer to XIAO

  1. Solder red wire → D2 pin on XIAO
  2. Solder black wire → any GND pin on XIAO

8 Visual inspection before flashing

  • ☐ No solder bridges between adjacent pins
  • ☐ Battery polarity correct (double-check)
  • ☐ All joints shiny and solid
  • ☐ No bare wire touching anything it shouldn't
  • ☐ Clean flux residue with isopropyl + cotton swab

8b Pre-flash electrical test (do this before touching a computer)

Set multimeter to Ω mode, 200 range. The GDT-311 has no beeper — watch the display.

  1. Test each solder joint: probe the wire above the joint on one side, probe the board pad on the other. Should read near 0. If it reads OL or high, reflow the joint.
  2. Test switch continuity: probe the two button wires (at any exposed metal — connector pins, stripped sections). Unpressed = OL or ~2.0. Pressed = near 0. If it reads near 0 unpressed, the switch is shorted — replace it.
  3. Test no shorts: probe D1 pad to D2 pad. Should read OL. Probe any signal pad to GND unpressed. Should read OL (D1) or near 0 (GND-to-GND).
If you can't hold probes + press the switch at the same time, skip the switch continuity test here and verify it in Step 10 via the logs instead.

9 Flash ESPHome firmware

  1. Open ESPHome dashboard on your laptop
  2. Copy the correct YAML from repo — e.g. button-ryker-laundry.yaml
  3. Confirm secrets.yaml has WiFi credentials and API key filled in
  4. Click Install → Plug into this computer
  5. Connect XIAO via USB-C
  6. Wait for compile + flash (~2–3 min)
  7. Watch logs for: WiFi connected → API connected → Button handler registered
  8. Disconnect USB-C
If flash fails: hold BOOT button on XIAO while connecting USB-C to force bootloader mode.

10 First press test (before enclosure)

  1. Flash the device and open logs
  2. Confirm GPIO1 is reading correctly: with logs streaming, briefly short the D1 and GND wires together. You should see Button pressed in the logs. If not — stop here and troubleshoot before proceeding.
  3. Press the physical tact switch
  4. Confirm in logs: Button pressed → HTTP POST sent → response received
  5. Buzzer should beep on press
  6. XIAO returns to deep sleep within 10 seconds
{"status": "outside_window"} = working fine, just not during the task window
{"status": "already_done"} = working fine, task already logged today

If shorting D1+GND works but physical switch press doesn't: the solder joint on the switch is the problem. Reflow both switch joints and retest.

11 Assemble in enclosure

(After 3D print is ready and mechanically tested)
  1. Place XIAO in slot with USB-C aligned to enclosure opening
  2. Route battery alongside/below XIAO
  3. Mount tact switch under plunger post — confirm it actuates at bottom of plunger travel
  4. Mount buzzer against inside wall, aligned with sound grille
  5. Close enclosure
  6. Press plunger — confirm tact switch clicks at bottom of travel

12 Label and mount

  1. Apply kid sticker + task icon to plunger face
  2. Test WiFi signal at mounting location (hold button there, watch logs)
  3. Clean wall surface with isopropyl alcohol, let dry
  4. Peel Command strip backing, press to back of enclosure
  5. Press button to wall for 30 seconds
  6. Do one final press test from mounted position

SymptomLikely causeFix
XIAO won't bootBad battery joint / shortCheck polarity, inspect solder
Won't connect to WiFiWrong SSID/password in secrets.yamlReflash with corrected secrets
Flash failsNot in bootloader modeHold BOOT + connect USB
No HTTP POST in logsPi server not running / wrong IPStart Pi server, check IP in config
LED flashes redTask already done todayExpected behavior — press tomorrow
No buzzer soundWrong pin / active buzzer usedConfirm D2, confirm passive buzzer
Switch not detectedCold solder jointShort D1+GND wires to test GPIO — if that works, reflow switch joints
GPIO never changes no matter whatWrong GPIO in firmware (old configs used GPIO3 which is an internal RF switch pin)Confirm firmware uses GPIO1 for D1 and GPIO2 for D2 — not GPIO3/GPIO4
